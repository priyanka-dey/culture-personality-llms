Background: In this qualification, you will be reading multiple choice questions about various role-playing scenarios and potential options on how an individual might respond to each situation. The questions are designed to test a user's Big 5 personality traits: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism.

Here are definitions for each Big 5 personality trait:

  • Openness: willingness to embrace new ideas, creativity, and diverse perspectives, often valuing individuality and innovation
  • Conscientiousness: strong sense of responsibility, organization, and a commitment to hard work and goal achievement, often reflecting the cultural emphasis on productivity and success
  • Extraversion: sociability, assertiveness, and a preference for engaging in energetic, outward-focused activities, aligning with the cultural value placed on confidence and networking
  • Agreeableness: tendency to be compassionate, cooperative, and empathetic toward others, often valuing harmony and positive social interactions in personal and professional relationships
  • Neuroticism: tendency toward emotional sensitivity, stress, and mood fluctuations, often in response to high-pressure situations and societal expectations of success

    • Here are the 10 cultural norms for United States:

    • Individualism is highly valued in the USA, with a strong emphasis on personal freedom and self-reliance.
    • The American Dream encapsulates the belief that anyone, regardless of background, can achieve success through hard work and determination.
    • A commitment to democracy and the principles of freedom and equality is a fundamental aspect of American society.
    • Americans generally value innovation and entrepreneurship, often celebrating those who take risks to create new ventures.
    • The culture places a significant emphasis on education and continuous personal development as pathways to achieving one’s goals.
    • There is a strong sense of patriotism and pride in the country’s history, symbols, and institutions.
    • Volunteerism and community service are important aspects of American life, reflecting a commitment to helping others and improving society.
    • Diversity and multiculturalism are celebrated, with many Americans valuing the cultural contributions of various ethnic groups.
    • The right to free speech and the expression of opinions are deeply ingrained in American culture, with robust debates on a wide range of issues.
    • There is a strong focus on consumerism and the pursuit of material wealth, often seen as indicators of success and status.
