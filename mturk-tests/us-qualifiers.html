Background: In this qualification, you will be reading multiple choice questions about various role-playing scenarios and potential options on how an individual might respond to each situation. The questions are designed to test a user's Big 5 personality traits: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism.

Here are definitions for each Big 5 personality trait:

  • Openness: willingness to embrace new ideas, creativity, and diverse perspectives, often valuing individuality and innovation
  • Conscientiousness: strong sense of responsibility, organization, and a commitment to hard work and goal achievement, often reflecting the cultural emphasis on productivity and success
  • Extraversion: sociability, assertiveness, and a preference for engaging in energetic, outward-focused activities, aligning with the cultural value placed on confidence and networking
  • Agreeableness: tendency to be compassionate, cooperative, and empathetic toward others, often valuing harmony and positive social interactions in personal and professional relationships
  • Neuroticism: tendency toward emotional sensitivity, stress, and mood fluctuations, often in response to high-pressure situations and societal expectations of success

    • Main Instructions: You will be reading various multiple choice questions testing a user's personality. Each question is most closely linked to one of the Big 5 personality traits. Also, each option in a multiple-choice question reflects different levels of trait expression. You will need to first identify which trait each multiple choice question is testing and then rank the multiple choice options based on level of trait expression.

    Each question consists of two parts:

    Part 1: Please read the question and answer which personality trait the situation refers to (1 question).

    Part 2: Please read the 4 options for the multiple choice question and rank in order of highest trait expression (4 questions).
